About the Paris

Introduction

A parish is a local church community that belongs to a larger diocese or ecclesiastical territory. It serves as a place of worship, community gathering, and religious education for its members. You are probably reading this document because your parish has become vacant and you need to produce a parish profile. The parish profile is a key part of the process of attracting a new priest to work with you as you seek to serve the needs of your church, community and the Gospel. The parish profile is the document on which a prospective applicant will base their decision to take the application further. If you are to attract the right person, then it needs to be an appealing document.

History

The concept of a parish dates back to early Christianity when communities began to form around local churches. Parishes are vital in organizing religious services, education, and charity within local neighborhoods. When you attend Mass in the Parish Center, or even in the Historic Little Church, it is hard to imagine that St. Joseph’s Parish has its roots in the year 1856. Yet, that is when this Catholic community celebrated its first Mass in a tiny log cabin just off the shores of Medicine Lake. St. Joseph’s began as a mission parish for mostly French-Canadian settlers.

Significance

Parishes play an important role in fostering a sense of belonging among their members. They often become central to community life, offering support through spiritual guidance, social activities, and outreach programs.

Community Activities

Many parishes engage in a variety of activities including mass, youth programs, charity work, and social events. These activities help strengthen the bonds among community members and encourage participation in church life.
